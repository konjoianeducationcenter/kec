Study Skills

Studying is an essential part of your school career. There are also many methods and skills required for studying. We offer programs to help you with time management and organizational skills. The regular program has two parts (listed separately below) and there is another one-part program for middle school.
  • Part 1
  • Part 2
  • Middle School

  • The Curriculum: Study Skills Part 1

    • This program will run for ten sessions with each session lasting (1) hour
    • The sessions are 1 on 1
    • The program will cost $1,250 for the ten sessions

  • Program Curriculum

    • Identifying areas of strengths and weaknesses
    • Organization skills
    • Note-taking from lectures and from textbook
    • Making a test study guide
    • Setting appropriate and attainable goals
    • Writing skills
    • How to read for comprehension (before, during, and after reading skills)
      • Textbook comprehension (science, social studies, etc.)
      • Novel, short story, ELA reading comprehension

  • We employ a variety of organizational tools as part of our curriculum. These include:

    • Mock lectures
    • Mock science lab excercises
    • Recall note taking strategy
    • Fostering active learning
    • Outlined for papers

  • The Curriculum: Study Skills Part 2

    • This program will run for five sessions with each session lasting (1) hour
    • The sessions are 1 on 1
    • Weekly subject follow up and calendar process
    • The program will cost $550 for the five sessions

  • Program Curriculum

    • Class Scheduling and Planning (Calendar)
    • Subject A Strategy
    • Subject B Strategy
    • Subject C Strategy
    • Course Evaluations

  • The Curriculum: Middle School Study Skills

    • This program will run for ten sessions with each session lasting (1) hour
    • The sessions will be 1 on 1
    • The program will cost $1,000 for the ten sessions

  • Program Curriculum

    • Week 1: Organizing the Student
    • Week 2: Identifying Strengths and Weaknesses
    • Week 3: Modifying the Environment
    • Week 4: The Locker vs. The Backpack
    • Week 5: Time Management
    • Week 6: Making Time Work (Planning)
    • Week 7: Building Sustained Attention
    • Week 8: Goal Direction/Oriented
    • Week 9: Expect the Unexpected
    • Week 10: Recap/Review
